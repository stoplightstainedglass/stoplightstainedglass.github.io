I started my stained glass journey around 5 years ago. One evening, while my now, mother-in-law was working on some glass herself and I mentioned I had always had an interest in making stained glass, and of course how beautiful it was. I explained to her how my uncle and great-aunt use to do it, but unfortunately both had both passed by the time my interest had peaked. She said she would happily teach me and off we went!

Stained glass is a true art and I was unaware of how much time it took. Learning the practices, precision and reward has all taken an outrageous amount of time and patience.

Here we are 5 years later and I would not change it for anything. I have learned so much and developed my craft. I do a wide range of items, the smallest of being pendants and necklaces and the largest thus being a custom panel for a bathroom.

What started as pure fun, soon showed the chance of also becoming an income. I've started attending numerous festivals and events selling my pieces all over the tri-state area. For me this has always been a family affair. My wife helps me with patterns and layouts, my parents, grandmother, and in-laws help me at shows and my brother is my tech support.

Donovan Leonard 2011-2016
